Topics to Study

Legislation accounts for ~25% of the Animals and Public Policy domain (≈ 6 questions on the 350-item exam).

  • Federal
    Animal Welfare Act (AWA)
    Federal scope and enforcement by USDA-APHIS; what the AWA does and does NOT cover — companion animals and free-ranging animals are excluded. Key regulated activities: research, exhibition, transport, dealing.
  • Federal
    PACT Act 2019
    Preventing Animal Cruelty and Torture Act — first federal law explicitly protecting companion animals from cruelty. Criminalizes crushing, burning, drowning, suffocating, impaling, or seriously harming animals. Penalties up to 7 years federal imprisonment.
  • State/Local
    Breed-Specific Legislation (BSL)
    Ordinances restricting or banning specific breeds (most commonly "pit bull"-type dogs). AVMA, ASV, and HSUS oppose BSL on evidence grounds; Calgary model (owner accountability) cited as effective alternative. Know preemption laws — some states prohibit local BSL.
  • State/Local
    Retail Pet Sale Bans & Puppy Mill Regulations
    State and local ordinances prohibiting pet stores from selling dogs/cats from commercial breeders; "adopt, don't shop" mandates. Enforcement varies; some require shelter-source-only sales.
  • State/Local
    Anti-Tethering / Chaining Laws
    Laws restricting continuous or unattended tethering of dogs. Vary greatly by state; some impose time limits, weather restrictions, or tether-type requirements.
  • State/Local
    TNR / Community Cat Ordinances
    Legal status of Trap-Neuter-Return programs varies by jurisdiction; some explicitly authorize, others prohibit abandonment (cats returned to field may be deemed abandoned). Know your state's position and AVMA guidance on community cats.
  • State/Local
    Model Dog & Cat Control Ordinances
    NACA and HSUS model ordinances as frameworks for progressive shelter management. Cover licensing, impound procedures, adoption requirements, dangerous dog protocols, and TNR provisions.
  • State/Local
    State vs. Local Preemption Laws
    Some states preempt local governments from enacting breed-specific or other stricter animal control ordinances. Critical to understand when advising shelters on policy advocacy.
  • State/Local
    Mandatory Microchipping Laws
    Growing number of jurisdictions require microchipping at adoption or for certain breeds. Shelter-level implications: scanning on intake, compliance reporting, owner reunification duties.

⚠ Likely Exam Traps

  • The AWA does NOT protect companion animals — it regulates research, exhibition, transport, and commercial dealing only
  • The PACT Act (2019) was the FIRST federal law to protect companion animals from cruelty — not the AWA
  • BSL opposition is the consensus position of AVMA, ASV, and HSUS — questions may test whether you know the professional stance, not just the law
  • Returning cats to field under TNR may constitute "abandonment" in jurisdictions without explicit TNR authorization
  • State preemption laws can prohibit local BSL even if local officials support it
