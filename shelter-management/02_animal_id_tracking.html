Animal ID and Tracking

Individual record requirements, the 9-item intake dataset, identification triad, in-shelter care tracking, and intake/outcome statistics. Verified against ASV Standards of Care 2022 and SAC BasicDataMatrix 2021.

ONENOTE notes ASV Standards 2022 SAC 2021

The ID Mandate

The most fundamental rule in shelter record-keeping: every single animal entering the shelter system must have a unique identifier and an individual record, established at or prior to admission.

The core rule ASV 2022

  • Unique: No two animals may share an identifier. Two dogs of the same breed, color, and age must have separate, distinct IDs.
  • Individual: Each animal requires its own record. A litter of kittens housed together still requires one individual file per kitten.
  • Timing: The ID and record must be established at or prior to admission, not after intake processing is complete.
  • Purpose: To ensure consistency and accuracy in care and record-keeping from the animal's first moment in the system.
Exam Trap: ID cannot be delayed ID establishment cannot be deferred until after the animal is sorted, treated, or assigned a housing location. It must exist at or before the moment of admission. Any care provided before an ID is assigned lacks a traceable record, which is a compliance failure regardless of emergency circumstances.

Why individual records matter ASV 2022

FunctionWhy a unique individual record is required
Clinical continuity Every treatment, procedure, and medication is tied to one specific animal. Shared records allow medication errors and missed diagnoses.
Epidemiological tracing During a disease outbreak, housing locations logged by time for each individual animal allow the team to reconstruct the exposure chain. Group records make this impossible.
Legal and liability protection Individual records document compliance with holding periods, rabies quarantine requirements, and welfare standards. Group records cannot satisfy legal audit requirements.
Outcome accuracy Live release rates, ALOS, and Asilomar category data all depend on accurate individual-level records. Group files corrupt population-level statistics.
Unknown history management If a stray animal's history is unknown, the record must explicitly state "unknown" for those fields. Blank fields are non-compliant; documented unknowns are compliant.
Exam Trap: "Unknown" is a valid entry; blank is not For stray animals with no documented history, every mandatory field must still be populated. Fields where information is unavailable must state "unknown" or "not available." Leaving a field blank implies the data was simply not collected, which is a different and more serious compliance failure.

Consequences of non-compliance ASV 2022

  • Group records (e.g., "Litter 404B"): Each kitten in the litter must have a separate ID and record. Tracking a litter under one collective file violates the individual record mandate.
  • Delayed ID assignment: Any care provided before ID assignment cannot be reliably attributed to the correct animal, creating both clinical and legal risk.
  • Duplicate IDs: Two animals sharing one ID renders both records unreliable and makes outbreak investigation impossible.
  • Incomplete records: Missing any of the mandatory data fields (e.g., no sex documented, no weight/BCS recorded) constitutes a compliance failure for that record.

The Animal Record

A complete individual record consists of three data groups collected at intake: the nine animal information items (Phase A), the identification triad (Phase B), and the history dataset (Phase C).

Phase AAnimal Information (9 mandatory items)
  1. Unique identifier (number and/or name assigned by the shelter)
  2. Intake type (stray, owner surrender, OIE, transfer in, return, born in care)
  3. Date of intake
  4. Species
  5. Age or age category (SAC cutoff: under 5 months = pediatric)
  6. Sex
  7. Spay-neuter status
  8. Physical description (coat color, pattern, breed/mix, distinguishing features)
  9. Weight or Body Condition Score (BCS)
Exam Trap: Count to 9 Know all 9 items and be able to identify which one is missing from an incomplete record. Weight/BCS is the most commonly omitted item in exam scenarios. Sex and spay-neuter status are separate items (not one combined field).
Phase BIdentification Triad (legally binds animal to record)
  1. Photograph of the animal taken at intake (not optional; explicitly mandated)
  2. Microchip scan results (document as positive with chip number, or negative; must be scanned regardless of whether a chip is expected)
  3. Identifying markings (unique physical characteristics: scars, brands, tattoos, asymmetric coat patterns)
Exam Trap: Microchip scan RESULTS, not just chip status The required entry is the scan result (the specific chip number if present, or "no chip detected"). A policy that stops requiring photographs to save storage space is explicitly non-compliant; photographs are a mandated component of the identification triad.

In-Shelter Tracking

Three tracking mandates govern every day of an animal's shelter stay, plus the outcome documentation that closes the record. Each serves a distinct clinical, operational, or epidemiological function.

Phase C: History (collected at intake) ONENOTE notes

History data is gathered from the surrendering owner, transfer paperwork, or previous rescue records. For strays with no known owner, all fields must be populated with "unknown" rather than left blank.

#History itemWhy it matters for clinical care
1Medical conditions and services receivedIdentifies chronic conditions, current medications, and prior diagnoses that inform intake exam and pathway planning
2Behavioral events and experiencesPrior bites, fear responses, training history, and known triggers; informs handling protocols and track assignment
3People and animals lived withHousehold composition, tolerance for children, compatibility with other pets; directly influences adoption matching
4Home environmentIndoor/outdoor access, noise level, activity level; helps predict shelter adjustment and guides enrichment planning
5Vaccination history, specifically rabies vaccination historyRabies exposure and quarantine decisions depend on this. Rabies vaccination status must be explicitly documented; it is not sufficient to record general vaccine history without addressing rabies.
Exam Trap: Rabies vaccination history is specifically called out The ASV standards and your ONENOTE notes both explicitly flag rabies vaccination history as the one vaccine history item that must be specifically documented. General "vaccination history" is required, but rabies status within that history is called out by name because it drives quarantine and bite-case legal obligations.

In-shelter care activities (updated continuously) ASV 2022

1Medical Findings, Treatments, and Procedures
  • All physical examination findings, diagnoses, and diagnostic test results
  • All treatments administered: medications (drug, dose, route, frequency, dates), vaccinations given in-shelter, anthelmintics, flea treatments
  • All surgical procedures: pre-op findings, procedure performed, anesthetic protocol, post-op monitoring and medications
  • Ongoing monitoring notes for animals with active medical conditions
2Behavioral Findings, Plans, and Treatments
  • Daily behavioral observations: stress indicators, appetite, interaction quality, kennel cough behaviors (barrier frustration, spinning, pacing)
  • Formal behavioral assessments: dates, assessors, results
  • Behavior modification plans: target behaviors, techniques, assigned staff
  • Progress notes: response to interventions, plan updates, reassignment between fast and slow track
  • Any incident reports: bites, near-misses, aggression events, with handling recommendations updated
3Housing Locations by Time
  • Every housing location must be logged with the start and end date (and ideally time) the animal occupied it
  • Examples: "Intake kennel 3A, June 1 to June 3 | Medical ward room 2, June 4 to June 8 | Adoption floor run 12, June 9 onward"
  • This is the most critical tracking parameter for infectious disease outbreak investigation
  • Off-site locations count too: foster homes, off-site adoption events, transport holding
Why housing location by time is irreplaceable for epidemiology When a canine influenza outbreak is detected, the shelter team must identify every animal that was in the same airspace as the index case during the relevant incubation window. Only time-stamped housing records make this possible. Group records or records without time stamps render contact tracing impossible, converting a manageable outbreak into a shelter-wide crisis requiring full quarantine. Source: ASV Standards of Care 2022.

Outcome documentation (closes the record) ONENOTE notes

Two fields are required to officially close a shelter stay record:

FieldWhat to documentExamples
Outcome typeThe specific exit pathwayAdoption, return to owner, transfer to rescue, return to field, foster placement, euthanasia, died in care, lost in care
Date of outcomeThe exact calendar date the animal left the shelter's care"March 5, 2026" (not "early March" or "this week")

Both fields must be populated. An outcome type without a date (or a date without an outcome type) is an incomplete record. Outcome date drives ALOS calculations; outcome type drives live release rate and Asilomar category reporting.

Intake and Outcome Statistics

Your ONENOTE notes direct intake/outcome statistics to the epidemiology section. This tab covers the SAC data framework, key metrics derived from individual records, and how accurate ID tracking enables meaningful analysis.

SAC intake categories SAC 2021

The Shelter Animals Count BasicDataMatrix standardizes six intake types for shelter reporting. Each maps to the "intake type" field in Phase A of the individual record.

Intake categoryDescriptionKey exam point
Stray / at largeAnimal found or brought in from the community as a straySubject to legal holding period before any disposition
Owner surrenderOwner voluntarily relinquishes the animal to the shelterNo mandatory stray hold; owner signs relinquishment
Owner-Intended Euthanasia (OIE)Owner brings animal specifically for euthanasiaDistinct SAC category; not bundled with owner surrender
Transfer inAnimal received from another shelter or rescueArriving records must be incorporated into individual file
ReturnPreviously adopted animal returned by adopterOriginal record is reopened, not a new record created
Born in careAnimal born to a dam already in the shelter's careEach neonate requires its own individual record from birth
Exam Trap: OIE is not owner surrender Owner-Intended Euthanasia is a distinct SAC intake category. Counting OIE animals as owner surrenders overstates surrender numbers and distorts outcome statistics (since OIE performed is the expected and appropriate outcome for these animals). Key performance metrics derived from individual records ASV 2022

Average Length of Stay (ALOS)

ALOS = Sum of all individual animal LOS values / number of animals in the cohort. Each animal's LOS = outcome date minus intake date. Without accurate intake dates and outcome dates in individual records, ALOS cannot be calculated.

ALOS should be tracked separately by species, age category, intake type, and track (fast vs. slow) to identify which subpopulations are driving extended stays.

Live Release Rate (LRR)

Three distinct calculation methods exist (see population management file). All three require accurate individual outcome type data. The SAC BasicDataMatrix uses Method 1: Live outcomes / Total live intakes.

LRR is most meaningful when reported within Asilomar categories. An overall LRR of 95% means very different things depending on whether the remaining 5% are Healthy, Treatable, or Unhealthy-Untreatable.

Return rate and recidivism

  • Return rate = number of animals returned / total adopted in a period. Requires tracking that each animal returned had previously been adopted from this shelter.
  • A high return rate is not inherently negative; it may reflect a shelter culture that encourages returns rather than abandonment. Context matters.
  • Comparing return rates across shelters requires knowing each shelter's return policy and adopter support programs.

How individual record accuracy enables program evaluation ASV 2022

Analysis goalRecord field(s) required
Identify which intake type has the longest ALOSIntake type (Phase A) + intake date + outcome date

Assess impact of a new foster program on ALOSHousing locations by time (foster home entries) + outcome date

Exam Trap: Garbage in, garbage out All shelter statistics are only as reliable as the individual records they derive from. If intake type is inconsistently entered, if outcome dates are approximated, or if housing locations are unrecorded, every metric calculated from those fields is unreliable. Accurate individual records are the non-negotiable foundation of meaningful shelter data analysis.
