Record Keeping

Digital infrastructure standards, dual reporting requirements (individual records and population-level reports), and goal-assessment functions of shelter software systems. Verified against ASV Standards of Care 2022.

ONENOTE notes ASV Standards 2022

The Digital Standard

Paper-based systems are inadequate for modern shelter management. Digital systems, preferably those designed specifically for animal shelters, are the required standard.

The core directive ONENOTE notesASV 2022

  • Digital systems must be used for record keeping. This is the standard, not a preference.
  • Preferred: software systems designed specifically for animal shelters. Shelter-specific software is engineered to handle the unique data needs of animal welfare organizations, including intake categories, outcome types, Asilomar classifications, and housing tracking.
  • Also acceptable: structured spreadsheet programs. Spreadsheet tools can accomplish the core functions when shelter-specific software is not available or affordable.
  • Not acceptable: paper ledgers, cage cards alone, verbal tradition, or physical whiteboards as the primary record system.

What shelter software manages ONENOTE notes

Shelter software or spreadsheet programs allow organizations to better manage three operational domains:

  • Resources: staffing hours, veterinary supply inventory, surgical suite scheduling, transport vehicle availability, funding allocations
  • Schedules: daily cleaning rotations, medication rounds, surgical lists, adoption appointment slots, population rounds, foster check-in schedules
  • Shelter processes: intake workflow, holding period tracking, pathway assignment (fast/slow track), outcome processing, transfer coordination
Exam Trap: Shelter-specific software is preferred, but spreadsheets are acceptable The standard does not require shelter-specific software at all costs. Spreadsheet programs are a recognized and acceptable alternative. The key distinction is digital vs. paper, not shelter-specific vs. generic. A well-structured spreadsheet is compliant; a paper-based system is not.

Dual Reporting Requirement

A compliant shelter database must support two distinct types of data output. A system that can do only one of the two is functionally deficient.

The two required outputs ONENOTE notesASV 2022

The software system used by a shelter must be able to generate both of the following:

  1. Individual animal records that track a single resident's complete clinical, behavioral, and logistical journey from intake to outcome
  2. Basic population-level reports that compile shelter-wide aggregate metrics for operational management, transparency, and epidemiological purposes

Goal Assessment and Reporting

The record-keeping system must actively support the organization's ability to assess progress against its stated goals. Data that cannot be interrogated cannot drive improvement.

The assessment mandate ONENOTE notes

  • The record-keeping infrastructure must allow regular assessment and reporting of organizational goals and activities.
  • This is not a passive function: the system must actively enable leadership to interrogate data and evaluate progress.
  • Regular assessment allows administrative leadership to dynamically review operational progress, justify funding allocations, and adjust capacity thresholds based on real-time data.

How goal assessment connects to strategic planning ASV 2022

  • The strategic plan sets measurable targets (e.g., reduce feline ALOS by 10% over Q3).
  • The record-keeping system generates population-level reports that show whether the target is being met.
  • Strategic plans are reviewed quarterly; this review requires the database to produce relevant metrics on demand.
  • Without a system that allows regular goal assessment, the strategic plan is an inert document rather than an active management tool.

Exam Trap: Goal assessment requires population-level reports, not individual records Evaluating progress toward an organizational goal (e.g., "Did our feline LRR improve?") requires population-level aggregated data, not individual animal records. An individual record tells you what happened to one cat. A population report tells you what happened across all cats in the cohort. The exam may present scenarios requiring you to identify which data type is needed.

Resource and schedule management as a goal-assessment tool ONENOTE notes

  • Shelter software that manages resources and schedules also supports goal assessment indirectly: by ensuring resources are being used efficiently, it helps the organization operate within C4C and maintain the population density required to achieve LRR targets.
  • For example: if the surgical suite schedule shows 3-week waits for spay/neuter, this is a bottleneck that will extend LOS and suppress live release rates. The system identifies the problem; leadership can then act on it.
  • Resource tracking also supports grant reporting: funders often require detailed accounting of how resources were deployed, which a digital system can generate far more reliably than a paper record.
